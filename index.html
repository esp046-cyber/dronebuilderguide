Complete Drone Building Guide for Filipinos

Build your own racing or camera drone step by step

What You Need to Know

Building your own drone in the Philippines is cheaper than buying ready-made. You can customize it for racing, photography, or just fun flying. This guide uses plain English and focuses on parts available in Manila, Cebu, and online stores that ship to PH.

Budget Range:
Basic drone: PHP 8,000 - 12,000
Mid-range: PHP 15,000 - 25,000
Advanced: PHP 30,000+

What Type of Drone to Build

  • Racing Drone: Fast, agile, needs FPV goggles
  • Freestyle Drone: For tricks and aerial flips
  • Cinematic Drone: Smooth camera shots, stable flight
  • Long Range: Fly far, longer battery life

Essential Parts List

These are the main components you need. All available in PH through Lazada, Shopee, or hobby shops in Greenhills and Gilmore.

1. Frame

The skeleton that holds everything together. Made of carbon fiber for strength and light weight.

Size: 5 inch is most common (refers to propeller size)

Popular choices:

  • Martian II - PHP 1,500-2,000
  • QAV-R - PHP 2,500-3,500
  • Generic Chinese frames - PHP 800-1,200
Frame: X-shaped carbon fiber base with arms

2. Motors (You Need 4)

Brushless motors that spin the propellers. Size written like 2205, 2306, etc.

For 5 inch drone: Use 2205-2306 size motors

KV rating: Higher KV = faster speed, lower flight time

  • Racing: 2300-2600 KV
  • Freestyle: 1900-2300 KV
  • Cinematic: 1700-1900 KV

Price: PHP 600-1,200 each (buy 4)

Brands: Emax, T-Motor, BrotherHobby, RCinPower

Motor: Round metal cylinder with rotating shaft

3. ESC - Electronic Speed Controller (You Need 4)

Controls motor speed. Modern drones use 4-in-1 ESC (all in one board) or individual ESCs.

Important specs:

  • Current rating: 30A, 35A, 40A (higher for bigger motors)
  • Firmware: BLHeli_S or BLHeli_32 (32 is better)
  • For 5 inch: Use 30-35A ESC

4-in-1 ESC: PHP 1,800-3,500

Individual ESC: PHP 300-600 each (buy 4)

ESC: Small electronic board with wires

4. Flight Controller (FC)

The brain of the drone. Stabilizes flight and processes your commands.

Popular firmware: Betaflight (most common), INAV, Kiss

F4 vs F7: F7 is faster processor, better for GPS and complex setups

Price: PHP 1,500-3,500

Many come in stack with ESC: FC + ESC combo saves space

Flight Controller: Small circuit board with chips

5. Radio Receiver (RX)

Receives signals from your controller. Must match your transmitter protocol.

Protocols:

  • FrSky (most popular in PH)
  • FlySky (budget friendly)
  • TBS Crossfire (long range)
  • ExpressLRS (new, very good, cheap)

Price: PHP 600-2,500

Receiver: Small box with antenna wire

6. FPV Camera

The camera you see through while flying. Not for recording, just for real-time view.

Specs:

  • Size: Micro (19mm) or Mini (28mm)
  • Sensor: CMOS or CCD (CMOS is standard now)
  • Look for: Good low-light performance, wide dynamic range

Popular: Runcam, Foxeer, Caddx

Price: PHP 1,200-2,500

FPV Camera: Small camera with lens and circuit board

7. Video Transmitter (VTX)

Sends camera feed to your goggles. Uses 5.8GHz frequency.

Power: 25mW to 800mW (higher = longer range but drains battery)

For Philippines: 25mW-200mW is legal and enough for most flying

Price: PHP 800-2,000

Important: VTX needs antenna always connected or it will burn out instantly when powered on!
VTX: Small board with antenna connector

8. Battery (LiPo)

Lithium Polymer battery that powers everything. Most critical and dangerous part.

For 5 inch drone:

  • Size: 4S (14.8V) or 6S (22.2V)
  • Capacity: 1300-1800 mAh
  • Connector: XT60 is standard
  • C-rating: 70C or higher

Price: PHP 1,200-2,500 each

Buy at least 3-5 batteries for decent flying time

Battery Safety:
- Never leave charging unattended
- Store at 3.8V per cell
- Use fireproof bag
- Check for puffing or damage
- Dispose properly if damaged
LiPo Battery: Rectangular pack with wires and connector

9. Propellers (You Need Many)

You will crash and break these. Buy at least 10 sets.

For 5 inch frame: Use 5 inch props

Pitch: 3-blade or 4-blade

  • 3-blade: More efficient, longer flight time
  • 4-blade: More grip, better for freestyle

Price: PHP 150-400 per set of 4

Popular: HQ Props, Gemfan, DAL

Propellers: Plastic blades in CW and CCW rotation

Building Your Drone Step by Step

Follow these steps carefully. Take your time, especially with soldering.

Before You Start:
- Work in well-ventilated area
- Watch YouTube build tutorials for your specific frame
- Test continuity with multimeter after each solder
- Take photos at each step for reference
- Never plug in battery until all checks done
1 Prepare the Frame

Unpack frame and organize all screws and standoffs. Clean carbon fiber with alcohol to remove dust. Install standoffs on bottom plate using screws.

2 Mount the Motors

Install motors on each arm using screws provided. Motor screws go from bottom up. Check motor direction - CW motors on diagonal corners, CCW on other diagonal. Tighten screws in star pattern.

3 Prepare ESCs

If using 4-in-1 ESC: Mount on standoffs above motors. If using individual ESCs: Mount one on each arm near motor. Leave motor wires long for now.

4 Solder Motors to ESCs

Cut motor wires to appropriate length (not too short). Strip about 3mm insulation. Tin motor wires and ESC pads with solder. Solder motor wires to ESC pads. Any order is fine, you will swap later if motor spins wrong direction.

Soldering Tips:
- Heat the pad and wire together
- Apply solder to joint, not iron tip
- Joint should be shiny when done
- Use enough solder but not too much
- Each joint takes 2-3 seconds
5 Install Power Distribution

If ESC has PDB built-in: Skip this step. If not: Mount PDB on standoffs. Solder ESC power wires (red and black) to PDB pads. Keep wires organized.

6 Install Flight Controller

Mount FC on top of ESC using soft rubber standoffs (dampens vibration). Align arrow on FC with front of drone. Connect FC to ESC using ribbon cable or wires depending on your stack.

7 Solder XT60 Battery Connector

Solder XT60 male connector to battery power wires from ESC or PDB. Red wire to positive, black to negative. Slide heat shrink over before soldering. Solder capacitor across XT60 pads (watch polarity - negative stripe to negative).

Never short positive and negative together! This will spark and damage components.
8 Install VTX and Camera

Mount camera on front using camera mount. Connect camera to VTX using camera cable. Mount VTX on stack or on side of frame. Connect VTX to FC for smart audio (optional). Solder VTX power to 9V or 12V pad on FC or PDB.

9 Install Receiver

Mount receiver on frame with double-sided tape or zip tie. Connect receiver to FC using SBUS or CRSF protocol. Check pinout diagram for FC and RX. Usually: RX signal to FC TX pad, RX ground to FC ground, RX power to 5V pad.

10 Antenna Installation

Attach antennas to VTX. Use zip ties to mount antennas vertically or in V shape on back arms. Keep antennas away from carbon fiber and metal. Antennas should be at angle for better reception.

11 Cable Management

Use zip ties to secure all wires. Keep wires away from propellers. Route wires neatly along arms. Cut excess zip tie length. Apply heat shrink to any exposed solder joints.

12 Final Assembly

Install top plate. Attach battery strap. Check all screws are tight (use Loctite on motor screws). Install propellers (check rotation direction). Make sure nothing is touching that should not be touching.

13 Pre-Flight Checks

Visual inspection of all solder joints. Check for shorts using multimeter (test positive to negative - should read open circuit). Check motor screws are tight. Verify propellers are on correct motors and right way up. Test VTX has signal before connecting battery.

Software Setup and Configuration

Now you need to configure the flight controller using computer.

What You Need

  • Computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
  • USB cable (micro USB or USB-C depending on FC)
  • Betaflight Configurator (download from betaflight.com)
1 Install Betaflight Configurator

Download from betaflight.com. Install on your computer. Open the program.

2 Connect FC to Computer

Connect FC to computer using USB cable. Do NOT connect battery yet. Select the correct COM port in Betaflight. Click Connect button.

3 Flash Firmware (if needed)

Go to Firmware Flasher tab. Select your FC board type. Select latest stable Betaflight version. Click Load Firmware Online then Flash Firmware. Wait for completion.

4 Configure Ports

Go to Ports tab. Enable Serial RX on the UART where your receiver is connected. Set protocol to match your receiver (SBUS, CRSF, etc). Save and Reboot.

5 Configuration Tab

Set mixer type to Quad X. Enable Motor Stop (for safety). Set minimum throttle. Enable Air Mode. Set up battery monitoring (cells, voltage). Save settings.

6 Configure Receiver

Go to Receiver tab. Power transmitter ON and bind to receiver following receiver instructions. Check channel map matches your transmitter. Adjust endpoints if needed. Test all sticks and switches work.

7 Setup Modes

Go to Modes tab. Assign ARM switch (usually 3-position switch). Setup flight modes: Angle mode for beginners, Horizon for learning, Acro for full control. Setup Beeper for lost model. Save settings.

8 Motor Direction and Order

Go to Motors tab. Read warnings and check propellers are OFF. Connect battery (be careful). Test motors at low throttle one by one. Check they spin correct direction. If wrong, swap any 2 motor wires. Motors should be: Front Right = Motor 1, Back Right = Motor 2, Back Left = Motor 3, Front Left = Motor 4.

9 PID Tuning (Basic)

Go to PID Tuning tab. For first flight, use default PIDs. After test flight, adjust if drone wobbles or feels loose. Increase P gains if too soft. Decrease if oscillating. This takes practice.

10 OSD Setup (Optional)

Go to OSD tab. Enable on-screen display elements you want: battery voltage, timer, RSSI, altitude. Position elements on screen. Save settings.

Save Your Settings: Go to CLI tab, type "dump all", copy all text and save to file. This backs up your configuration.

Learning to Fly

Do not rush. Flying FPV drone takes practice. Start slow and safe.

Before First Flight

Safety First:
- Fly in open area away from people
- Check weather - no rain or strong wind
- Inform people nearby you are flying
- Have fire extinguisher for battery fire
- Wear safety glasses
- Never fly near airport or government buildings

Pre-Flight Checklist

  • Check all screws tight
  • Propellers installed correctly (check direction and tightness)
  • Battery fully charged and not puffed
  • Camera angle adjusted properly
  • VTX has good video signal in goggles
  • Controller batteries fresh
  • Receiver connected and responding
  • GPS has good satellite lock (if applicable)
  • Clear flying area of obstacles

Flight Mode Explanation

Angle Mode (Stabilized)

Drone self-levels when you release sticks. Best for beginners. Cannot flip or do acrobatics. Drone will not go upside down.

Horizon Mode

Self-levels at center stick but allows flips at full stick. Good for learning acro gradually.

Acro Mode (Rate/Manual)

No self-leveling. Full manual control. This is what racing pilots use. Most difficult but most control. Start in Angle mode first.

First Flight Steps

1 Setup and Power On

Place drone in open grass field. Connect battery. Wait for flight controller to initialize (usually 5-10 seconds). Put on goggles and verify video feed.

2 Arm the Drone

Check transmitter switches are correct. Flip ARM switch. Propellers should start spinning slowly. If not arming, check Betaflight for error messages.

3 Hover Practice

In Angle mode, slowly increase throttle to 50%. Drone should lift off ground. Practice holding steady hover at knee height. Use small stick movements. Practice for 10 minutes before moving around.

4 Forward/Backward/Sides

Once hovering is stable, practice moving forward slowly then back. Then left and right. Keep movements small and controlled. Practice figure-8 patterns.

5 Landing

Reduce throttle slowly until drone touches ground gently. Disarm immediately. Do not leave armed on ground.

Learning Progression

Week 1-2: Master hovering and basic movements in Angle mode

Week 3-4: Practice flying boxes, figure-8, and orientation flying

Week 5-6: Try Horizon mode, practice flips at high altitude

Week 7+: Gradually transition to Acro mode, start with hovering again

Simulator Practice: Use Liftoff, DRL Simulator, or Velocidrone on PC to practice before real flying. Connect your transmitter to computer. This saves money on crashes.

Common Problems and Solutions

Problem Solution
Drone flips on takeoff Motor direction or propeller wrong. Check motor order.
Oscillation/wobbling PIDs too high or props damaged. Lower P gains or replace props.
Drifting in Angle mode Accelerometer not calibrated. Calibrate in Betaflight.
Weak punch out Battery weak or ESC/motor issue. Check connections.
Video static or loss Antenna damaged or VTX too hot. Check antennas and airflow.
Won't arm Check Betaflight error messages. Common: no RC signal, gyro not calibrated.

Philippines Drone Laws and Regulations

You must follow CAAP (Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines) rules.

Drone Registration (Required)

All drones must be registered with CAAP if:

  • Weight more than 7 kg - Need RPA Certificate
  • Used for commercial purposes - Need operator permit
  • Recreational use under 7kg - Still register online

Register at: CAAP website or office

Cost: Around PHP 300-500 for recreational

Flying Rules in Philippines

Strictly Prohibited:
- Within 10km of any airport
- Over crowds or public events
- Near Malacanang or government buildings
- Military installations or bases
- Over 400 feet altitude
- At night without special lights
- When visibility is poor

Where You Can Fly

  • Private property with permission

Maintenance and Care

After Every Flight

  • Check for loose screws
  • Inspect propellers for cracks
  • Clean dirt from motors and frame
  • Check battery voltage and condition
  • Store batteries at storage voltage (3.8V per cell)

Monthly Maintenance

  • Tighten all screws (use Loctite)
  • Check solder joints for cracks
  • Clean camera lens
  • Check antenna connections
  • Update firmware if available
  • Backup Betaflight settings

Battery Care

Critical Battery Rules:
- Never discharge below 3.5V per cell
- Store at 3.8V per cell (storage charge)
- Charge in fireproof bag or container
- Never leave charging unattended
- Check for puffing before each use
- Dispose properly at electronic waste center if damaged

Crash Recovery

After a crash:

  1. Disarm immediately if still armed
  2. Disconnect battery
  3. Check for broken propellers (replace)
  4. Check motor screws tight
  5. Test each motor spins freely by hand
  6. Check camera and VTX not damaged
  7. Check for loose wires
  8. Test hover before flying normally

Troubleshooting Flowchart

Drone Won't Arm

Check in this order:

  1. Is transmitter ON and bound? Check transmitter power and binding
  2. Does Betaflight show RC signal? Check receiver connection and wiring
  3. Check Betaflight error messages Fix specific error shown
  4. Is gyro calibrated? Calibrate accelerometer in Betaflight
  5. Is ARM switch correct? Check Modes tab in Betaflight

Drone Flips on Takeoff

Check in this order:

  1. Check motor order (1-4 correct positions)
  2. Check motor directions (CW vs CCW)
  3. Check propeller direction (look for arrows on props)
  4. Check props are on correct motors

Video Feed Problems

Check in this order:

  1. Is VTX antenna connected? Connect antenna
  2. Is VTX powered? Check voltage on VTX pad
  3. Is camera connected? Check camera cable
  4. Wrong channel/band? Match VTX and goggles frequency
  5. Antennas damaged? Replace antennas
